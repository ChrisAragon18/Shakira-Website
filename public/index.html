About Shakira

Shakira Isabel Mebarak Ripoll was born in Baranquilla, Colombia on February 2, 1977. She has a Colombian mother, a Lebanese father, and 8 older half siblings. Growing up Shakira was mesmerized seeing her father using his typewriter to write stories, she eventually gets one at the age of 7 and began to write poetry which eventually turned into songs. Belly dancing was another art she took interest in due to her Lebanese descent. She would dance on tables and even at school every Friday, she was known as the "belly dancer" girl. Even at a young age, she managed to make herself known through her love for music and art. Her talent brought her many opportunities and she even joined talent competitions.

Shakira's History

Her First Song

At the age of two, one of Shakira's brothers passed away in a motorcycle accident. Due to this incident, she wrote her first song as a tribute to her father's grief of this loss called "Tus Gafas Oscuras" which translates to "Your Dark Sunglasses." The lyrics speak about her fathers suffering over the loss of his son and how the shades he wore obscured him and made him feel mysterious and distant. This made Shakira feel hurt and confused as she was too young to fully comprehend her fathers grieving. The song wouldn't be officially released until 1991 for her first album "Magia."

Impact on Colombia During Political Turmoil

Shakira's uniqueness and music brought hope and harmony to the country of Colombia that was in disarray in the 1990's. A Marxist-Leninist guerrilla group, known as the Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia (founded in 1964), was active during the 90's and would initiate bombings, kidnappings, and other violent acts. The guerrilla group's purpose was to fight against the Colombian security forces over the staggering levels of inequality during that time. As violence ensued throughout the country, Shakira's music would be played on radios, restaurants, and cassette players known as Walkman's. She was the driving force for uplifting the spirits of Colombians and a motivational base for perseverance.

Hips Don't Lie

In 2006, Shakira released her single, "Hips Don't Lie," that topped the charts in 50 countries, a milestone that hadn't been accomplished by a Latin singer previously. It is a Latin pop and reggaeton song that Shakira wrote alongside Wyclef Jean, Jerry Duplessis, Omar Alfanno and LaTavia Parker. Jean was tasked to remix one of Shakira's songs, "La Tortura,"" but he had felt that it was the wrong project for the right artist. The rapper had an intuition that because the Latin sensation had a gift for belly dancing, it could propel his tune to new heights. He turned out to be right. "Hips don't lie" ended up being #1 on the music billboard (the most she had climbed was #6) and not just in America but other countries such as the UK, France, and Germany. During the week of its release, it had been played 9,637 times on American radio setting the national record for most-played pop tune in a single week.

What Shakira Has Done for the World

In 1997, Shakira herself founded her own charity called the "Fundacion Pies Descalzos" (translated: "Barefoot Foundation"). The famous singer did this with an objective of promoting quality public education for children in need in her home country Colombia. Not only does it provide education for children, but the foundation also funds the children's families by giving them a better home. Shakira is frequently visiting the kids and their families to encourage them not to give up and is always giving them inspiration to help others and continue going on. Colombia was not the only location she provided for either. In 2003, Shakira became a goodwill ambassador for UNICEF (United Nations International Children's Emergency Fund) to internationally bring awareness to children in third-world countries. Shakira strongly believes in what she does, referencing a quote from Global Citizen: "I really feel from delivering education around the world, universally, equally, so many positive consequences come, from economic growth to development, to stability. If we really want a world with peace, with global stability, we have to increase the investment in education."
